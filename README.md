# Personal Exam Experience

> **AWS Certified AI Practitioner (AIF-C01)**
>
> ✅ Passed with **78%**

## My Takeaways

I passed the AWS Certified AI Practitioner exam with a score of **78%**. Based on my experience, the topics covered in these notes were sufficient for the majority of the exam questions.

### Topics I Found Most Important

* Amazon Bedrock
* Foundation Models (FMs)
* RAG and Knowledge Bases
* Prompt Engineering
* Bedrock Guardrails
* Amazon Q
* SageMaker basics
* AI/ML fundamentals (Supervised, Unsupervised, RLHF, Bias vs Variance)

### Amazon Nova is Important

One area that surprised me was the number of questions related to the **Amazon Nova model family**. I encountered approximately **3–4 questions** specifically about Nova models.

### Exam Tips

* Pay attention to **cost optimization** scenarios.
* Understand when to choose **Nova Lite** versus **Nova Pro**.
* Expect questions around **video generation**, where **Nova Reel** is the correct answer.
* Be comfortable identifying the most **cost-effective**, **lowest-latency**, or **highest-performance** model for a business requirement.
* Several questions are scenario-based and test your ability to select the appropriate AWS AI service rather than memorize definitions.

## Final Advice

Focus on understanding AWS AI services and their use cases rather than memorizing every feature. Most exam questions are business-scenario driven and require choosing the best service based on requirements such as:

* Cost
* Latency
* Accuracy
* Scalability
* Security
* Ease of implementation

These notes, combined with hands-on familiarity with Amazon Bedrock, Amazon Q, and SageMaker concepts, should provide solid coverage for the exam.
